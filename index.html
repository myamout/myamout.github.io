Llamas the majestic animal of South America

The llama is a relative of the camel that calls the Andes Mountains home. Our great furry friends were domesticated by the native population in South America due to their ability to carry large loads of supplies while navigating the rocky mountain terrain. Llamas can carry up to 75 pounds whilst traveling 20 miles a day!


Llamas are strictly herbivores, eating mainly grass. Like their camel cousins, llamas can survive off of a large variety of plants and can go for long periods without water. These traits help them adapt to the mountain environment they live in.


Despite being domesticated as a mode of transporting goods, llamas are most certainly not all work and no play! Llamas have been known to refuse to move if the load on their backs is too heavy! If unhappy with their work load they will simply flop on the ground and lay there until the load is lightened. Make sure you treat your llama right as they helped pave the way for the great South American civilizations. Llama power has indeed moved mountains!


Interesting Llama Facts

Llamas can run up to 35 mph

Though it may not look like it at first, llamas can run extremely quickly! Its been theorized that our furry friends lie in wait and unlease their speed when the time is right. Video evidence has been collected of llamas doing practice runs.

The scientific name for llama is Llama glama

This fact may shed some light on the name of website, but yes the scientific classification name for llamas is Llama glama! As the name may suggest all llamas are indeed glamorous. When alone most llamas are busy working on their hairstyles between bites of grass!

Humming is a common way llamas communicate with one another

Llamas are great musicians it may seem as the most popular way they "talk" to each other is through humming. Watch out, that next big hit might just feature a llama!

Llamas are very intelligent and can be trained

What's that? Llamas can be trained? Move over fido because man just found a new best friend. Llamas are extremely smart and are able to learn things quickly as well as communicate effectively with eat other in the herd. Just the perfect side kick to have by your side when you are trying to survive the robot latest robot uprising.
